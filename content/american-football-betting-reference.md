## Basic Rules

### Match Structure
- Four quarters of 15 minutes each (60 minutes game clock)
- Half-time break after the 2nd quarter
- 2-minute warning at the end of the 2nd and 4th quarters (clock stops)
- Real-time duration is typically 3+ hours due to stoppages
- Clock stops for: incomplete passes, out of bounds, penalties, timeouts, change of possession, scores, injuries, 2-minute warning

### Scoring
| Method | Points |
|---|---|
| Touchdown (TD) | 6 |
| Extra point (PAT kick) | 1 (after TD) |
| Two-point conversion | 2 (after TD) |
| Field goal (FG) | 3 |
| Safety | 2 (awarded to defense) |

### Gameplay Basics
- 11 players per side on the field
- Offense has 4 downs (attempts) to advance 10 yards
- If successful, they get a new set of 4 downs
- If they fail, possession turns over to the other team
- Teams typically punt on 4th down to push the opponent back

### Key Positions
- **Quarterback (QB)**: Leads the offense, throws/hands off the ball
- **Running Back (RB)**: Carries the ball on rushing plays
- **Wide Receiver (WR)**: Catches passes downfield
- **Tight End (TE)**: Hybrid blocker/receiver
- **Defensive Line / Linebackers / Secondary**: Defensive units

### Key Rules
- **Forward pass**: Only one allowed per play, must be thrown from behind the line of scrimmage
- **Fumble**: Ball dropped by a player in possession — live ball, either team can recover
- **Interception**: Defensive player catches a forward pass
- **Pass interference**: Illegal contact with a receiver while the ball is in the air
- **Holding**: Illegally grabbing/restricting an opponent
- **Instant replay/challenge**: Coaches can challenge certain calls (limited challenges per game)

## Overtime Rules

### NFL Regular Season
- One 10-minute overtime period
- Coin toss determines first possession
- If the first team scores a touchdown, they win immediately
- If the first team scores a field goal, the other team gets a possession
- If still tied after the period, the game ends in a tie

### NFL Playoffs
- Same format but continues until there's a winner (no ties)
- Multiple overtime periods if needed

## Season Structure

### NFL Regular Season
- 18 weeks, 17 games per team
- 32 teams split into 2 conferences (AFC / NFC), each with 4 divisions of 4 teams
- Season runs September through early January

### NFL Playoffs
- 7 teams per conference qualify (4 division winners + 3 wild cards)
- Single elimination bracket
- Wild Card → Divisional → Conference Championship → Super Bowl
- Super Bowl is typically early February

### College Football (NCAA)
- Different overtime rules (each team gets possession from the 25-yard line)
- Longer season, more teams, conference-based with a playoff system
- Different clock rules (clock stops on first downs)

## Common Betting Markets

### Point Spread (Spread)
- The most popular NFL bet
- Favorite is handicapped (e.g., Kansas City **-3.5**)
- Underdog gets points (e.g., Buffalo **+3.5**)
- Bet wins if the team covers the spread
- **Push**: If the spread lands exactly (e.g., -3 and team wins by 3), bet is refunded

### Moneyline
- Simply pick the winner, no spread
- Expressed as odds (e.g., -150 favorite, +130 underdog)
- Less common in NFL than spread due to parity

### Over/Under (Totals)
- Combined score of both teams
- Typical NFL totals range from 37 to 55
- Can also bet team totals individually

### First Half / Second Half
- Spread, moneyline, and totals for each half separately
- First half is more predictable; second half is influenced by game flow adjustments

### Quarter Lines
- Spread and totals for individual quarters

### Props (Player & Game)
- **Passing yards**: QB over/under (e.g., Mahomes over 285.5 yards)
- **Rushing yards**: RB over/under
- **Receiving yards**: WR/TE over/under
- **Touchdowns**: Anytime TD scorer, first TD scorer
- **Completions, attempts, interceptions**
- **Sacks, tackles** (defensive props)

### Parlays
- Combining multiple bets into one — all must hit to win
- Same-game parlays (SGPs) are hugely popular in NFL

### Teasers
- Adjusted spreads on multiple games (move the line in your favor)
- Standard: 6-point, 6.5-point, 7-point teasers
- All legs must win

### Futures
- Super Bowl winner
- Conference/division winners
- MVP, Offensive/Defensive Player of the Year
- Win totals (over/under regular season wins)

### Live / In-Play
- Odds update after every play
- Next score type, drive result, real-time spreads and totals

## Key Concepts for Betting Context

### Key Numbers
- **3**: Most common margin of victory (field goal)
- **7**: Second most common (touchdown + PAT)
- **6, 10, 14**: Other significant margins
- Lines crossing these numbers significantly affect value

### Settlement Rules
- Bets settle on **final score including overtime** (unless market specifies "regulation only")
- If a game is postponed and played within 48 hours, bets typically stand
- Abandoned/cancelled games: bets voided unless the result is already determined

### Injuries & Inactives
- NFL teams release injury reports (Wednesday–Friday during the week)
- Final inactive list 90 minutes before kickoff
- Player props are voided if the player doesn't participate

### Weather
- Outdoor stadiums are affected by wind, rain, cold
- High wind = fewer passing yards, lower totals
- Dome teams are unaffected

### Home Field Advantage
- Historically worth ~2.5–3 points in the spread
- Has diminished in recent years but still a factor

### Scheduling Factors
- **Thursday Night Football**: Short week, often lower-scoring
- **London/international games**: Travel fatigue, neutral site
- **Bye weeks**: Teams coming off byes tend to perform better
- **Divisional matchups**: Often tighter games regardless of record

### Turnovers
- Interceptions and fumble recoveries drastically swing games
- Turnover margin is the #1 predictor of game outcome
- Difficult to predict, which is why spreads exist

## Terminology Quick Reference

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ATS (Against the Spread) | Win/loss record when accounting for the spread |
| Cover | Beat the point spread |
| Push | Result lands exactly on the spread (bet refunded) |
| Juice/Vig | The bookmaker's commission (typically -110) |
| Hook | The half-point on a spread (e.g., 3.5 vs 3) |
| Backdoor cover | Covering the spread with a late, meaningless score |
| Garbage time | Late-game play when outcome is decided |
| Pick 'em | No spread — both teams considered even |
| Trap game | Overlooked opponent after/before a big matchup |
| Reverse line movement | Line moves opposite to where most bets are placed |
| Sharp money | Bets from professional/informed bettors |
| Steam move | Rapid, significant line movement |
| Teaser | Parlay with adjusted (more favorable) spreads |
| Middle | Betting both sides at different numbers to win both |
| Bad beat | Losing a bet due to a last-second unlikely event |
